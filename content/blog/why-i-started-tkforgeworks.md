Every side project starts with the same dangerous thought: "How hard could it be?"

TK ForgeWorks began as a name I gave to the corner of my desk where personal projects lived. You know the spot — the one with the half-assembled prototype, three notebooks full of ideas, and a coffee stain that's been there so long it's practically a design feature.

## The Day Job

By day, I'm a mechanical engineer herding cross-functional teams to bring transit systems to life. It's rewarding work — there's something deeply satisfying about seeing infrastructure you helped design carrying actual people around actual cities. But it comes with the usual corporate package: customer requirements, regulatory approval, and meetings about meetings about the meetings we had last week.

## The Escape Hatch

TK ForgeWorks is where I get to ask "what if?" without submitting a change request form. Where I can overengineer a kitchen organizer just because I want to. Where I can decide to build an entire action RPG as my first game development project and have nobody around to tell me that's an absurd scope for a solo developer with no art skills.

The name itself is half-serious, half-aspirational. "TK" for my initials. "ForgeWorks" because I liked romanticizing the idea of a one-person workshop where raw materials become useful things through sheer stubbornness. Like a blacksmith, except instead of horseshoes, I'm making things that occasionally compile.

## What's Here

This site is the digital version of that desk corner. You'll find:

- **Projects** in various states of ambition and completion
- **Blog posts** documenting what I'm learning (and what I'm learning the hard way)
- **An FAQ** that preemptively answers questions nobody has asked yet

I'm documenting the journey — the wins, the failures, and especially the moments where I realize my initial time estimate was off by a factor of three. Because if there's one thing I've learned from engineering, it's that the most interesting discoveries happen when things don't go according to plan.

Welcome to the forge. Mind the sparks.
